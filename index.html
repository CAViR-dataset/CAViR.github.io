Abstract


Video anomaly retrieval aims to localize anomalous events via natural language queries for public safety. Existing datasets are limited by data scarcity (due to long-tail anomalies) and privacy constraints. We introduce the Cross-modal Anomaly Video Retrieval (CAViR) benchmark, a large-scale synthetic dataset generated to overcome these challenges. CAViR contains 41,315 videos (1.36M frames) with matched captions covering 30 normal and 68 anomalous events (e.g., falling, fighting). To validate learning from synthetic data, we propose the Flow-guided Language-video Alignment with Mixture-of-Experts (FLAME) framework. FLAME incorporates two modules: (1) Token-Aware Mixture-of-experts (TAM) for fine-grained spatial representation via token-aware experts for normal/anomaly behaviors; (2) Cross-modal Flow Predictor (CFP) linking motion text with visual dynamics using optical flow for temporal modeling. Experiments show FLAME achieves state-of-the-art anomaly retrieval on CAViR and real-world UCFCrime-AR, with text-to-video Recall@1 of 58.5% and 41.0%, respectively.

Benchmark


Pipeline of our Cross-modal Anomaly Video Retrieval (CAViR) benchmark construction. First, we collect and generate diverse video descriptions via LLM. Second, we leverage a state-of-the-art open-source video generative model to craft high-quality videos. Third, we adopt LLM to assign preliminary attributes for samples lacking explicit normal/anomaly labels and refine all labels by K-Means clustering and manual verification. After augmenting each video with four additional captions in distinct styles, the final dataset integrates 206,575 rigorously curated video-text pairs.

Method


Overview of our FLAME (Flow-guided Language-video Alignment with Mixture-of-experts) framework, which contains two core modules: (b) Token-Aware Mixture-of-experts (TAM) for fine-grained spatial representation via token-aware experts distinguishing normal and anomalous behaviors, and (c) Cross-modal Flow Predictor (CFP) linking motion text with visual dynamics through optical flow for temporal modeling. The framework is jointly optimized by optical flow prediction loss and video-text cross-modal alignment objectives.
